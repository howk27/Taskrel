# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Taskrel is for solo and small trade contractors, usually one to ten people, who work from the field and need a phone-first way to create quotes, schedule work, invoice clients, and keep records connected. The primary user is non-technical, busy, and often checking the app between job-site tasks, client calls, and estimates.

## Product Purpose

Taskrel helps contractors move from a professional quote to scheduled work, invoice, payment, and export without needing a heavy field-service platform. The first wedge is AI-assisted quote creation with contractor-controlled pricing and consistent client-facing quote documents. Success means a contractor can create, review, send, follow up, convert, and invoice with confidence from a mobile screen.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, legible, professional, and practical. Taskrel should feel like a dependable workbench, not a marketing dashboard. Dark mode exists primarily for eye comfort and focus, but the interface should still use clear hierarchy, readable contrast, and task-specific surfaces instead of becoming one flat dark board.

## Anti-references

Avoid decorative marketing clutter, novelty illustrations, busy dashboard chrome, vague analytics-first layouts, and dark-on-dark interfaces where every panel blends together. Do not make Taskrel feel like a complex enterprise dispatch suite or a generic SaaS dashboard. Avoid quote flows that hide whether a quote is still a draft, ready to send, already sent, approved, or ready for invoice.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the next job to be done.
2. Make quote status and submission state impossible to miss.
3. Separate editing, review, sending, and follow-up as distinct workflow moments.
4. Keep dark mode calm and readable, with contrast and surfaces chosen for field use.
5. Favor operational clarity over decorative polish.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls, support reduced motion, and design mobile-first at 390px before desktop refinement. Touch targets should be comfortable for field use, and color should never be the only way to understand quote status or urgency.
